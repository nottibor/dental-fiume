FAQs

Q: What is Invisalign and how does it work?

A: Invisalign is a modern orthodontic treatment that uses a series of clear, removable aligners to straighten teeth. These aligners are custom-made for each patient and gradually shift your teeth into the desired position over time. You wear each set of aligners for about two weeks before moving on to the next set in the series.

Q: What is the price of Invisalign clear braces?

A: The cost of Invisalign treatment can vary based on the complexity of your case and the duration of your treatment. On average, price starts from â‚¬1,900. During your initial consultation, your dentist will provide a detailed estimate based on your specific needs and discuss available payment plans or financing options to help make the treatment more affordable.

Q: How long does treatment with Invisalign take?

A: The length of Invisalign treatment varies depending on the complexity of each case, but most patients complete their treatment within 12 to 18 months. During your initial consultation, your dentist will provide a more accurate estimate based on your specific needs.

Q: Are Invisalign aligners comfortable to wear?

A: Yes, Invisalign aligners are designed to be comfortable. They are made from smooth, BPA-free plastic and fit snugly over your teeth. While some patients may experience minor discomfort or pressure when switching to a new set of aligners, this usually subsides within a few days.

Q: Can I eat and drink while wearing Invisalign aligners?

A: You should remove your Invisalign aligners before eating or drinking anything other than water. This helps prevent staining and damage to the aligners. After eating, be sure to brush your teeth before reinserting the aligners to maintain good oral hygiene and keep the aligners clear.

Q: How often do I need to visit the dentist during Invisalign treatment?

A: Regular check-ups are essential to ensure your treatment is progressing as planned. Typically, you'll visit your dentist every 6 to 8 weeks for brief appointments. During these visits, your dentist will monitor your progress and provide you with the next sets of aligners.
